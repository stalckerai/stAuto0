If You Can’t Explain Yield, You Are the Yield.

DeFi made yield easy to see. But it made it much harder to understand.

Dashboards show numbers.
APYs update in real time.
Returns appear to compound.

But most users never ask the most important question:

Where is that yield actually coming from?

This week, we want the community to explore a deeper idea:

In markets, if you don’t understand the source of your return — you’re often the one providing it.

Use the framework below to guide your thinking.

1️⃣ Start With the Illusion

Open with how yield is presented in DeFi today.

-- high APYs on dashboards
-- simple deposit → earn flows
-- minimal explanation behind returns

Then introduce the core tension:

Yield looks simple on the surface, but the reality underneath is often much more complex.

2️⃣ Break Down the Gap Between Displayed and Real Yield

Explore why the number shown is often misleading.

You can discuss:

-- gross vs net return
-- impermanent loss
-- rebalancing costs
-- execution friction
-- volatility impact

Explain how a high APY can compress significantly when these factors are considered.

3️⃣ Explain Where Yield Actually Comes From

This is a key section.

Discuss the real sources of yield:

-- trading fees
-- lending activity
-- arbitrage
-- liquidations
-- incentives / emissions

Not all yield is equal.

Some is sustainable. Some is temporary.

4️⃣ Introduce the Idea of Hidden Value Transfer

Explore the concept:

If you don’t understand the system, you may be the one subsidizing it.

You can frame this as:

-- providing liquidity without understanding risk
-- earning incentives while absorbing downside
-- participating without modeling outcomes

This is where your title comes to life.

5️⃣ Explain Why Outcomes Differ

Discuss why different participants get different results.

For example:

-- some users optimize for APY
-- others analyze structure, cost, and risk
-- institutions model before deploying capital

Same system, different outcomes.

The difference is understanding.

6️⃣ Introduce the Shift Toward Engineered Yield

Transition toward the future.

DeFi is evolving from:

yield chasing → yield engineering

Explain what this means:

-- modeling expected outcomes
-- managing risk
-- optimizing over time
-- focusing on net returns

End with a strong takeaway:

Yield is not just a number.

It is:

-- revenue
-- minus cost
-- adjusted for risk

Understanding that changes how you approach DeFi entirely.
